Projects

A cabinet of small things I've made. A few are useful. Most exist because I couldn't leave them alone. It's all a bit self-indulgent, which is rather the point of a personal site.

Play these in your browser

I keep writing little games in C, Rust, Elm and Haskell, usually instead of finishing the previous one. WebAssembly means five of them now run right here, so you can judge for yourself. They share a design brief I found in an old README: everything is pushable, and optimise for OMG WOW moments. I can only really vouch for the first half.

▶ Recursive Sokoban

C · raylib · wasm

Boxes contain whole levels. Push one, step inside, solve the puzzle within, climb back out. Nests up to 16 deep.

▶ Functional Sokoban

C · raylib · wasm

Push value boxes through function boxes - +1, *2, NEG, INV. Later levels quietly teach you currying.

▶ Paint Machine

C · raylib · wasm

A small Zachtronics-ish factory: emitters feed colorers, cloners, portals and fans to paint bands onto a canvas. Play, step, rewind.

▶ Slime & Teleports

C · raylib · wasm

Slime trails make things slide and refuse to stop. Recursive slime, coloured teleport pairs, and a lighting system I did not need to add.

▶ Worm Division

C · raylib · wasm

A worm divides, and the clones replay your input history with relative orientation. Automation by accident. Now also has time machines.

▶ Asteroids

Elm

Compiles straight to JavaScript, so it's been quietly playable here for years - bugs and all.

These five grew out of one 685-commit repo that got away from me. In the history there's a level called 09_true_infinite.txt, a 05_curry.lvl titled "Currying: Make +5 Function", and a commit that just says "Put OMG TESTING level at the top". The main branch is mostly a segfault diary, including three separate reverts of a time machine.

chip8

C · wasm port pending

A CHIP-8 emulator with a clean, portable core and 36 real ROMs bundled - PONG, TETRIS, INVADERS, the usual suspects. Today it runs in a terminal; the browser port is on the list, somewhere below "tidy the shed".

Things that are alive on the internet

  • t8r8r - "fulfilling your potato-rating needs." An ELO ranking system. For potatoes. Commits include "Add cringey /about page" and "Make potatoes more boring". The joke is the product.
  • shortstories.lol - short stories from language models I fine-tuned on my own laptop back in the Llama 2 days. They're not good, and I nearly cooked the MacBook making them.
  • intuition - about 62 single-diagram maths proofs you can drag around and poke, plus a playground for Conway-operator polyhedra. I'm quietly fond of this one.
  • classiccult - a zero-backend site listing classic and cult films across a handful of London cinemas. A GitHub Action scrapes the listings once a day into a static JSON file. To get past one cinema's Cloudflare I piggyback a stranger's residential-Pi open-data project. Nothing to host, nothing to pay for.
  • choosetwo.org - trilemmas as SVG triangles: pick any two. CAP theorem, Zooko's triangle, Epicurus, and one for Career / Family / Hobbies. "Life is short; make a conscious decision how to spend it."
